ONTOLOGY — 7 Entity Types (assign exactly one)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. ACTIVE_SPECIES
   Redox-active molecules/ions, redox couples, supporting electrolytes, additives, solvent precursors, salt reagents.
   Examples: "VO²⁺", "Cr³⁺/Cr²⁺", "HCl", "FeCl₂·4H₂O", "bismuth nitrate", "TEMPO", "H₂SO₄"

2. MEMBRANE
   Ion-exchange membranes, porous separators, and modifications.
   Examples: "Nafion 117", "Nafion 212", "SPEEK/PBI blend"

3. ELECTRODE
   Electrodes, bipolar plates, current collectors. INCLUDES every named derivative state (see Rule 1 below).
   Examples: "graphite felt (GF)", "Bio-GF-O", "TBCC", "RCC"

4. CONDITION — requires subtype field:
   • "operating" — parameters during cell operation (temperature, current density, flow rate, SOC, cutoff voltage, cycle number, electrolyte volume ...)
   • "processing" — parameters during synthesis/pretreatment (annealing temperature, heating rate, atmosphere, precursor concentration, soaking duration ...)

5. METRIC — requires subtype field:
   • "performance" — cell-level indicators (CE, VE, EE, capacity retention, peak power density, cycle life, energy density ...)
   • "material_property" — intrinsic material characterization (BET surface area, contact angle, ID/IG ratio, Rct, ΔEp, IEC, conductivity, adsorption energy ...)

6. DEGRADATION
   Failure phenomena AND their underlying mechanisms, combined.
   Includes: crossover, capacity fade, HER / gas evolution, electrode passivation, electrolyte imbalance, decomposition, as well as mechanistic explanations.

7. SYSTEM
   Physical device / equipment components.
   Examples: "stack unit", "peristaltic pump", "tube furnace", "CHI 760E workstation", "CT-4008Tn testing system"

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
CRITICAL RULES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Rule 1 — Derived electrode names are SEPARATE entities.
  "RCC", "TCC", "BCC", "TBCC" → four ELECTRODE entities.
  "GF", "Bio-GF-O", "Bi-Bio-GF-O" → three ELECTRODE entities.
  Do NOT merge them. Record base material + treatment chain in key_attribute.

Rule 2 — Chemical identity precision.
  Metal ≠ ion ≠ redox couple ≠ salt. Each is distinct:
  "Bi" (metal) ≠ "Bi³⁺" (ion) ≠ "BiCl₃" (salt)
  "Fe²⁺" ≠ "Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺" ≠ "FeCl₂·4H₂O"
  Note relationships in key_attribute when relevant.

Rule 3 — Numeric values MUST be split.
  CONDITION and METRIC entities must have separate value, unit fields.
  For ranges, use value_min + value_max. NEVER concatenate number and unit into a single string.

Rule 4 — Preserve original names, do not canonicalize.
  "石墨毡" stays "石墨毡"; "graphite felt" stays "graphite felt".
  Canonicalization is a downstream pipeline step.
